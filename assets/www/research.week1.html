Greetings everyone. Besides browser related features, HTML/CSS/JS alone cannot be used to implement native features. It must use a framework such as Phonegap. You can do things such as let the mobile browser know a phone number is being displayed, and they can press the phone number to open the phone app to place a call, and the same idea with email. However, you will not be able to say place a call from within your app. Native features such as the accelerometer, camera, compass, notifications, and contacts will need to be accessed using a framework such as PhoneGap.

Pairing features to apps really depend on the app itself. The camera, notifications, storage, geolocation, file, and capture, all work pretty well across social media platforms as well as many other ideas. For instance, I created a new app for this class that I have been wanting to build for awhile. It is an aquarium enthusiast app that will allow you to search threw profiles of fish and offer some important tools in helping you build and maintain your own aquariums. Notifications can help me remind users that they need to run a water test, camera, file, media, capture, and storage can help the user interact with the site and save data locally to help the app run faster and more efficient. However, something like geolocation and accelerometer won't really help to much with anything.

For my app, I plan to implement notifications, storage, capture, and camera at least as described above. Allowing users to be alerted of a water test, share pictures of their own aquarium, save fish profiles into their favourites and "tanks".
